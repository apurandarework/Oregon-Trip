🕐Open daily 7:30 AM – 9 PM
💰FREE · Free guided tours 1 PM daily (Memorial Day–Labor Day)

Portland Japanese Garden

12 acres with 8 garden styles, koi ponds, bonsai terrace, and a tea house. Acclaimed as the most beautiful Japanese garden outside Japan. Allow 1.5–3 hrs.

🕐Wed–Mon 10 AM – 6 PM · Closed Tuesdays
💰Adult $22.50 · Youth (6–17) $16.50 · Under 5 free
💡Book ahead online — sells out on sunny days
📍~11 mi · ~20 min · Washington Park
★★★★★ Arrive at open for fewest crowds

Interactive exhibits, a real Navy submarine, planetarium shows, and a giant-screen theater. The definitive rainy-day plan — plan at least 3–4 hours.

🕐Tue–Fri 9:30–5:30 · Sat 9:30–7 PM · Sun 9:30–5:30
💰Adult $22.50 · Youth (3–13) $15.50 · Planetarium/Sub +$7–10
📍~13 mi · ~22 min · Central Eastside
★★★★☆ Plan 3–4 hours minimum

Historic · Outdoor

Pittock Mansion

A 1914 French Renaissance château on the West Hills with panoramic views of Portland, Mt. Hood, and five Cascade volcanoes. Grounds free — museum interior $16.

🕐Mon & Wed–Sun 10 AM–5 PM · Tue noon–5 PM
💰Grounds FREE · Museum: Adult $16 · Youth $12
🥾Hike up via Lower Macleay Trail (~5 mi round trip)
📍~12 mi · ~22 min · NW Portland
★★★★★ Best panoramic view in Portland
WebsiteMaps

Outdoor

Forest Park & Witch's Castle

5,156 acres of urban forest with 70+ miles of trails. The mossy "Witch's Castle" ruin on the Lower Macleay Trail is one of Portland's most iconic spots.

🕐Open daily dawn to dusk
💰FREE · Bring waterproof shoes
🥾Lower Macleay → Pittock Mansion: 5 mi round trip · Moderate
📍~12 mi · ~20 min · NW Portland
★★★★★ Go early on sunny days

Wine · Day Trip

Willamette Valley Wine Country

World-famous Pinot Noir across 700+ wineries in rolling Dundee Hills. May is Oregon Wine Month — special tastings, live music, and vineyard events all weekend long.

🕐Most tasting rooms 11 AM – 5 PM · Some require reservations
💰Flights $20–$65/winery · Guided tours ~$95–$120/person
📍~35 mi Trek · ~45 min via OR-99W → Sherwood → Dundee
★★★★★ May = Oregon Wine Month!
WebsiteMaps

🌅 Full Day Trip
Coast · Day Trip

Cannon Beach & Haystack Rock

One of the world's 100 most beautiful places (National Geographic). Haystack Rock stands 235 ft tall with tidal pools full of starfish and puffins. The Goonies and Twilight were filmed here. Ecola State Park is steps away.

🕐Beach always open · Best at low tide for tidal pools
💰Beach FREE · Ecola State Park parking $5/day
💡Depart Portland by 8 AM on sunny weekends to beat traffic
📍~80 mi Trek · ~1.5h via Hwy 26 W → Hwy 101 S
★★★★★ Named one of Earth's 100 most beautiful places
WebsiteMaps

Food Cart Pod · Downtown SW Portland

Midtown Beer Garden

Portland's oldest food cart pod — running since the early 1980s and reinvented as a 300-seat beer garden after a 2023 renovation. Steps from the Pearl District. Bing Mi's savory jianbing crepes, Stretch the Noodle's hand-pulled noodles, and Tokyo Sando's Japanese sandwiches lead the lineup.

🕐Mon–Fri ~8 AM–3 PM · Sat–Sun ~10 AM–5 PM
💰$8–14 per person · Cards accepted
🍽Bing Mi (Chinese jianbing) · Stretch the Noodle · Tokyo Sando · 300-seat beer garden
📍~12 mi · ~20 min · SW 5th Ave, Downtown Portland
★★★★☆ Portland's oldest pod — operating since the 1980s

🌅 Morning / Afternoon
Food Cart Pod · N Mississippi Ave

Mississippi Marketplace

Portland's first purpose-built food cart lot — opened 2009 and credited with sparking the entire city's pod boom. On the same block as Prost Marketplace and the legendary Prost! bar. Eco-friendly design with bike corral and Japanese lace leaf maples. A true slice of Portland history.

🕐Daily ~8 AM–7 PM (varies by cart)
💰$7–15 per person · Cash + cards
🍽Rotating seasonal carts · Cajun · BBQ · Mexican · next to Prost! bar
📍~17 mi · ~27 min · 4237 N Mississippi Ave (same block as Prost Marketplace)
★★★★☆ The pod that started Portland's food cart revolution
