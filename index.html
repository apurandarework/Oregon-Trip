International Rose Test Garden

10,000+ rose bushes with sweeping Mt. Hood views. America's oldest public rose test garden — peak bloom lands right during your late-May visit.

🕐Open daily 7:30 AM – 9 PM
💰FREE · Free guided tours 1 PM daily (Memorial Day–Labor Day)
📍~11 mi · ~20 min · Washington Park
★★★★★ Peak bloom during your trip

Garden

Portland Japanese Garden

12 acres with 8 garden styles, koi ponds, a bonsai terrace, and a tea house. Acclaimed as the most beautiful Japanese garden outside Japan. Allow 1.5–3 hrs.

🕐Wed–Mon 10 AM – 6 PM · Closed Tuesdays
💰Adult $22.50 · Youth (6–17) $16.50 · Under 5 free
💡Book ahead — sells out on sunny days
📍~11 mi · ~20 min · Washington Park
★★★★★ Arrive at open for fewest crowds
🔬 All Day
Museum

OMSI – Oregon Museum of Science

Interactive exhibits, a real Navy submarine, planetarium shows, and a giant-screen theater. The definitive rainy-day plan — plan at least 3–4 hours.

🕐Tue–Fri 9:30–5:30 · Sat 9:30–7 PM · Sun 9:30–5:30
💰Adult $22.50 · Youth (3–13) $15.50 · Planetarium/Sub +$7–10
📍~13 mi · ~22 min · Central Eastside
★★★★☆ Plan 3–4 hours minimum
🌅 Morning / Afternoon
Historic · Outdoor

Pittock Mansion

A 1914 French Renaissance château on the West Hills with panoramic views of Portland, Mt. Hood, and five Cascade volcanoes. Grounds free — museum interior $16.

🕐Mon & Wed–Sun 10 AM–5 PM · Tue noon–5 PM
💰Grounds FREE · Museum: Adult $16 · Youth $12
🥾Hike up via Lower Macleay Trail through Forest Park (~5 mi)
📍~12 mi · ~22 min · NW Portland
★★★★★ Best panoramic view in Portland
🌅 Morning best
Outdoor

Forest Park & Witch's Castle

5,156 acres of urban forest with 70+ miles of trails. The mossy "Witch's Castle" ruin on the Lower Macleay Trail is one of Portland's most photographed spots.

🕐Open daily dawn to dusk
💰FREE · Bring waterproof shoes
🥾Lower Macleay → Pittock Mansion: 5 mi round trip · Moderate
📍~12 mi · ~20 min · NW Portland
★★★★★ Go early on sunny days
☀️ Afternoon / Evening
Landmark

Powell's City of Books

The world's largest independent bookstore — an entire city block, ~1 million books new and used. Free Sunday tours. Budget extra time; you will not leave quickly.

🕐Daily 10 AM – 9 PM · 365 days a year
💰FREE to browse · Free tours Sundays
📍~12 mi · ~20 min · Pearl District
★★★★★ A Portland essential

Largest continuously operating outdoor arts and crafts market in the US. Local artisans, live music, and street food along the Willamette waterfront. Open both Sat and Sun during your trip.

🕐Sat 10 AM–5 PM · Sun 11 AM–4:30 PM
💰FREE to enter
📍~13 mi · ~22 min · Old Town / Waterfront
★★★★★ Open both Sat & Sun during your trip

Lan Su Chinese Garden

A Ming Dynasty–style garden built by artisans from Suzhou, China — pavilions, a teahouse, and a koi lake in Portland's Chinatown. Allow 1–2 hours.

🕐Daily 10 AM – 5 PM (last entry 4:30 PM)
💰Adult $14 · Senior $11 · Youth (6–18) $9 · Under 6 free
📍~13 mi · ~22 min · Old Town / Chinatown
★★★★★ Most underrated spot in Portland

💰$1.50–$5 per donut · Cash accepted

Willamette River Dinner Cruise

Sunset on the Willamette with dinner, drinks, and the Portland skyline behind you. Portland Spirit runs the cruise — the perfect Saturday evening. Reserve well ahead.

🕐Typically 7 PM – 10 PM · Check Portland Spirit for schedule
💰~$85–$120 per person · Reservations required
📍~13 mi · ~20 min · Tom McCall Waterfront
★★★★☆ Book early — fills up on weekends

Wine · Day Trip

Willamette Valley Wine Country

World-famous Pinot Noir across 700+ wineries in rolling Dundee Hills. May is Oregon Wine Month — special tastings, live music, and vineyard events all weekend long.

🕐Most tasting rooms 11 AM – 5 PM · Some require reservations
💰Flights $20–$65/winery · Guided tours ~$95–$120/person
📍~35 mi Trek · ~45 min via OR-99W → Sherwood → Dundee
★★★★★ May = Oregon Wine Month!
